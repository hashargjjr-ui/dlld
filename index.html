// ABOUT THE PROJECT

Engineered to follow the sun

The Automatic Solar Tracker System is an intelligent electromechanical device designed to keep a solar panel perpendicular to the sun throughout the day. Using light-dependent resistors (LDRs) and a microcontroller, it continuously adjusts the panel's orientation to capture maximum sunlight — significantly outperforming stationary panels.

Real-time Sun Tracking
Responds to changing light conditions within seconds
Energy Efficient
Low-power servos only move when needed
Affordable & Scalable
Built with accessible components and open hardware

Project Objectives

  1. 01 Design a system that automatically aligns a solar panel with the sun's position.
  2. 02 Maximize solar energy output compared to fixed-position panels.
  3. 03 Use low-cost components and accessible microcontroller programming.
  4. 04 Demonstrate a scalable prototype for renewable energy applications.
// TRACKER CATEGORIES

Types of Solar Trackers

Different tracking systems optimized for various applications and environments.

Single-Axis Tracker

Rotates the solar panel on one axis (typically horizontal/east-west), tracking the sun's azimuth throughout the day.

+ Simple mechanical design and affordable
+ Easy to maintain and repair
+ 15-25% efficiency gain over fixed panels
− Cannot track seasonal elevation changes
Best for: Utility-scale farms, stationary installations

Dual-Axis Tracker

Tracks both horizontal (azimuth) and vertical (elevation) movements, keeping the panel perpendicular to the sun all day and across seasons.

+ Maximum solar energy capture
+ 30-40% efficiency gain over fixed panels
+ Works year-round effectively
− More complex, higher maintenance cost
Best for: Residential systems, experimental projects (our system!)

Fixed System

Stationary solar panels installed at a fixed angle, typically tilted toward the equator at the location's latitude angle.

+ Lowest cost and maintenance
+ Reliable and long-lasting
+ No moving parts to fail
− Cannot adapt to sun's movement
Best for: Budget-conscious installations, large arrays

Control Methods

Active Tracking (Our System)

Uses sensors (LDRs) and motors with microcontroller algorithms to continuously track sunlight. Requires power but delivers maximum efficiency.

Passive Tracking

Uses thermal expansion of fluids in tubes to shift weight and reposition. No electrical power needed, but less accurate and slower response.

// WORKING PRINCIPLE

How it works

A continuous feedback loop keeps the panel perfectly aligned with the brightest light source.

STEP 01

Sense

Four LDRs detect light intensity from different directions around the panel.

STEP 02

Compare

Arduino reads sensor values and calculates the direction of strongest sunlight.

STEP 03

Rotate

Servo motors adjust the panel on horizontal and vertical axes.

STEP 04

Harvest

Panel faces the sun directly, maximizing photovoltaic output.
